What happened
Agent: “Thank you for calling Kimble, how can I direct your call?”
Mike: “Hi, I have a question about an invoice on my account.”
Agent: “Sure, one moment please.”
Hold music begins at 0:35. Nobody returns. Mike disconnects after 74 seconds. No billing help offered. No payment taken.
📋 What this reveals
Operator has no billing access. A caller pressing 0 is routed to a general receptionist who can’t look up accounts. The only option is to transfer — and nobody picks up the transfer.
No dedicated AR/billing line in the IVR. The IVR menu covers refuse, limestone, coal, oil and gas — but zero billing option. A customer with an invoice question has no direct path.
No voicemail fallback. When the transfer fails, the caller is left on hold indefinitely. No option to leave a message, no callback. The call ends with zero resolution.

The Combined Effect
1 Call channel fails: Operator answers but cannot help. Hold music. Caller disconnects. No resolution.
2 Portal channel fails: Requires invoice number. Customer who can’t find their paper invoice is blocked. No guest pay. No email lookup.
3 Contact channel fails: Seven-field form including County. A customer trying to ask a simple billing question faces a bureaucratic intake process.

Result: a motivated customer who wants to pay has three blocked exits. Invoice ages. Friction turns into avoidance.
