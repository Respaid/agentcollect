Freight & Logistics Payment Challenges

  • Short-pays: Brokers and shippers pay less than the invoiced amount, deducting for accessorial charges, fuel surcharges, or penalties that were not agreed upon. A carrier invoices $3,500 for a load and receives $2,800 with no explanation. Chasing the $700 difference across hundreds of loads adds up to significant revenue loss.
  • Disputed loads: Shippers claim late delivery, wrong quantity, or damaged goods. The carrier performed the service but cannot collect because the dispute blocks payment. Each dispute requires BOL review, delivery receipt verification, and back-and-forth negotiation.
  • Accessorial charge disputes: Detention time, layover charges, lumper fees, and fuel surcharges are frequently contested. Shippers and brokers argue these charges were not pre-approved, even when the rate confirmation includes them.
  • Broker insolvency: When freight brokers go out of business or face cash flow problems, carriers are left holding unpaid invoices. The broker has the shipper's payment but has not passed it through. This is particularly common with small and mid-size brokers.
  • Factoring complications: Many carriers factor their receivables for immediate cash. When a factored invoice is disputed, the factor charges the carrier back, creating a double cash hit: no payment from the factor and no payment from the shipper.
  • Volume and velocity: A mid-size carrier runs hundreds of loads per week. Even a 2-3% dispute rate generates dozens of contested invoices monthly. Manual follow-up cannot keep pace.

How AgentCollect Recovers Freight Receivables

1. Documentation-based dispute resolution

Every freight dispute comes down to paperwork. The AI agent requests and cross-references rate confirmations, bills of lading, delivery receipts, and proof of delivery photos. When the documentation supports the carrier's invoice, the agent presents a clear, evidence-based case for payment. Ninety percent of disputes are resolved in the first interaction.

2. Short-pay recovery

For short-paid invoices, the AI agent identifies the discrepancy between the invoiced amount and the payment received, determines the reason for the deduction, and contacts the broker or shipper's AP department to recover the difference. Each short-pay gets individual attention, even when the amount is $200. Across hundreds of loads, these small recoveries add up to significant revenue.

3. Broker and shipper AP targeting

Freight invoices often go unpaid because they are stuck in the wrong queue. The AI agent identifies the actual accounts payable contact responsible for freight payments, bypassing generic inboxes and unresponsive contacts. Different person, different response rate.

4. Multi-channel outreach

Logistics AP departments are notoriously difficult to reach by email alone. The AI agent uses email, phone, and SMS in a coordinated sequence. Phone follow-up is particularly effective in freight, where AP teams process hundreds of invoices and prioritize whoever is most persistent.

5. Attorney-mode escalation

For brokers and shippers that ignore standard outreach, the AI agent escalates to attorney-mode communications. Licensed attorneys send formal demand letters with legal weight. Attorney-mode emails achieve a 70% open rate. For freight, this is especially effective because it signals the carrier is willing to pursue the claim legally.
