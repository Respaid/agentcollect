Scope & Methodology
Objective

To evaluate the effectiveness, security, and customer experience of Interstate Waste Services' accounts receivable collection process through simulated customer interactions.

Scope

The assessment covered inbound telephone interactions with IWS billing representatives, digital self-service portals, and dispute resolution processes. Testing was conducted during standard business hours (Eastern Time) at IWS's primary billing number: (866) 342-5497.

Methodology
PhaseDescription
Phase 1 Reconnaissance. Public information gathering on IWS customer base, billing processes, and digital properties. Identification of commercial customers with active waste collection accounts for use in simulated interactions.
Phase 2 Telephone Assessment. Three calls placed to IWS billing (866-342-5497) posing as representatives of known IWS commercial customers. Calls assessed identity verification protocols, data handling procedures, payment collection capabilities, and service quality. All calls were recorded.
Phase 3 Digital Assessment. Testing of all customer-facing payment portals referenced in IVR messaging, website navigation, and representative recommendations. Four URLs tested across two domains.
Phase 4 Dispute Simulation. One call placed as a customer with a legitimate billing dispute (missed dumpster pickup) to assess dispute resolution capabilities, customer retention protocols, and first-call resolution rates.
Assessment Framework

Each operational area was scored on a 0–10 scale based on industry best practices for AR collection operations. Ratings follow a standard audit classification: Satisfactory (7–10), Needs Improvement (4–6), Unsatisfactory (0–3).

Limitations

This assessment reflects a point-in-time evaluation. Results may not represent all customer interactions or all representatives. The assessment was conducted during standard business hours (Eastern Time). Portal testing was limited to publicly accessible URLs referenced by IWS staff or automated systems.

Detailed Findings
F-01 No Caller Identity Verification
CRITICAL
Condition Representatives disclosed full account details—including outstanding balances, payment histories, AP contact emails, equipment inventories, service schedules, and primary contact names—to unverified callers. No account number, callback verification, or security question was required across any of the three calls assessed.
Criteria Industry standard requires minimum two-factor verification before disclosing account information (e.g., account number + company name, or callback to registered number). NACHA and PCI-DSS guidelines mandate identity verification before sharing financial data.
Cause No standardized identity verification protocol appears to be in place. All three representatives relied solely on caller-provided company name and address to locate accounts. The consistency across representatives indicates a systemic gap rather than individual error.
Consequence $48,124 in account data disclosed across two vendor reconciliation calls. Data disclosed included: customer balances ($34,818.38 for CityMD; $13,305.68 for Chelsea Piers), AP email addresses, payment histories, primary contact names (e.g., Lizbeth Rutherford), equipment inventories (2x 30-yard dumpsters, 12x recycling bins, 5x trash containers), and daily pickup schedules. This creates exposure to social engineering, competitive intelligence gathering, and potential regulatory liability.
Corrective Action Implement mandatory two-factor verification protocol before any account data disclosure. Train all billing representatives on data classification and disclosure policies. Consider implementing automated identity verification at the IVR level prior to agent connection.
Evidence — Call 1: Assessor identified as CityMD office manager. Representative (Charlene) located account using only company name and address. Full balance ($34,818.38), AP email, payment history, and primary contact name disclosed within 15 minutes.
Evidence — Call 2: Assessor identified as Chelsea Piers representative. Representative (Sue) disclosed $13,305.68 balance, equipment inventory, daily pickup schedule, and multiple contact names. No verification attempted at any point.
F-02 No Payment Collection Capability
CRITICAL
Condition No payment was collected across any of the three calls. Representatives had no mechanism to accept payment during the call—no payment link, no card processing, no ACH capability. Customers requesting to pay were directed to a non-functional portal or told to mail a check.
Criteria Industry standard for AR billing operations includes real-time payment processing via phone (card or ACH), payment link delivery (SMS or email), and functional self-service portal. Leading waste management companies offer all three channels.
Cause No integrated payment processing system is connected to the billing team's workflow. Representatives lack tools to generate payment links or process payments during live calls.
Consequence $0 collected across all interactions despite engaged callers with outstanding balances totaling $48,124. Customers who call ready to pay are unable to do so, extending DSO and increasing write-off risk. With 10,000+ commercial customers, the estimated annual impact is $4.8M+ in delayed or uncollected receivables.
Corrective Action Implement phone payment processing (card and ACH). Enable representatives to generate and send branded payment links via SMS and email during calls. Deploy a functional online payment portal.

Detailed Findings
F-03 Dispute Resolution Failure
CRITICAL
Condition A caller reported a billing dispute (charged for a dumpster pickup that did not occur at Glen Pointe, 500 Frank W. Burr Boulevard). Representative Nancy confirmed account details without identity verification, then directed the caller to "speak with your sales rep, David." No case number was issued, no escalation was initiated, no follow-up was committed, and no timeline was provided.
Criteria Industry best practice requires first-call resolution capability for billing disputes, including: issuing a case/ticket number, documenting the dispute, providing an estimated resolution timeline, and escalating through a defined process. The billing team should have authority to issue provisional credits for service failures.
Cause The billing team appears to lack authority to resolve disputes or issue credits. No documented dispute escalation protocol was observed. Resolution depends on the customer successfully reaching a specific individual (David) through a separate call.
Consequence Customers with legitimate disputes receive no resolution, no case tracking, and no assurance of follow-up. This increases customer churn risk and extends payment delays. The customer in this scenario—disputing approximately $5,000—was left with zero resolution and a directive to call back and ask for someone by first name.
Corrective Action Establish a formal dispute resolution protocol with defined escalation tiers. Empower the billing team to issue provisional credits for documented service failures. Implement case numbering and tracking for all disputes. Define and communicate resolution SLAs to customers.
Evidence: Caller reported a missed dumpster pickup at Glen Pointe. Nancy (IWS) confirmed the address and equipment without verification, then stated: "You'll need to speak with your sales rep, David, about that." No case number, no escalation, no timeline, no follow-up. Call ended without resolution.
F-04 Customer Portal Non-Functional
CRITICAL
Condition The IWS interactive voice response (IVR) system directs customers to "use the MyIWS portal on our website" to manage accounts and make payments. The domain myiws.com is listed for sale ($2,695 on HugeDomains). Three additional portal URLs on the primary domain return 404 errors. Representative Charlene recommended an alternative portal ("BuildTrust") which also did not resolve to a functional payment page.
Criteria All customer-facing portal references (IVR, website, representative guidance) should resolve to functional, secure payment pages. Domain registrations for customer-facing properties should be maintained and monitored.
Cause The myiws.com domain registration appears to have lapsed. Website portal pages (/make-a-payment, /customer-portal, /myiws) do not exist or are not deployed. There is no apparent coordination between IVR messaging and actual digital properties.
Consequence Every customer who follows IVR or representative instructions to pay online is unable to do so. This forces all payment activity through phone channels that themselves cannot collect payment, creating a closed loop with zero payment capability.
Corrective Action Immediately secure the myiws.com domain. Deploy a functional payment portal at a consistent URL. Update IVR messaging to reference the correct, verified URL. Audit all customer-facing references (website, IVR, printed materials) for accuracy. Implement monitoring for domain expiration and portal uptime.

Risk Assessment & Impact Analysis
Estimated Annual Revenue Impact

Based on IWS's reported customer base of 10,000+ commercial accounts across the Northeast, industry-standard AR delinquency rates (typically 5–12% for commercial waste services), and the observed inability to collect payments through any channel (telephone, digital, or dispute resolution), the estimated annual revenue impact is $4.8M or greater in delayed or uncollected receivables.

This estimate is conservative. It does not account for customer churn driven by poor dispute resolution, reputational risk from data security gaps, or compounding effects of extended DSO on working capital.

Recommendations

# Recommendation Priority Finding
